Boundary & Topo Procedures

Boundary & Topo Procedures

Set a point on the F.D.O.T. Network and locate for a minimum of three minutes.

  • Tie all found property corners of the subject lot.
  • Tie all found PCPs along the street.
  • Tie all found property corners of adjacent lots.
  • If requested, reset property corners at coordinates provided by the office.

Set a nail at the center of the subject lot on the oppisite side of the road. This allows construction vehicles to enter and exit without disturbing the point.

  • Locate all improvements on the subject lot starting at the centerline of the road (including but not limited to AP, fences, risers, power poles, overhead power, etc).
  • Ground Shots on a 50 foot grid if possible.
  • Locate all drainage structures within 100 feet (including MES' and culverts).
  • Locate finish floor elevations of adjacent houses if a house is built.
  • Locate sanitary manholes or septic on adjacent lots. Locate water meters or wells on adjacent lots
  • Generally Find or set a nail in the nearest power pole. If a power pole does not exist within 100 feet, you may use the rim of a manhole, set an "X" cut in a drainage structure, or use a fire hydrant bolt.
  • Palm Coast Only The City of Palm Coast strongly recommends setting a nail in a power pole, even one across the road or a few lots away.
  • Palm Bay Only Locate the closest Palm Bay benchmark. If not provided, call the office or use this website to locate the closest benchmark.
  • Palm Bay Only Locate or set two nails in the road in front of the subject lot, one in front of each property corner.

Office Note: If a lot is on a curve, it is generally harder to tie the boundary to the monuments. Please take extra considerations when working on curved lots.
