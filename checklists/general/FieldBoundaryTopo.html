Boundary & Topo Procedures

Boundary & Topo Procedures

Set a point on the F.D.O.T. Network and locate for a minimum of three minutes.

  1. Tie all found property corners of the subject lot.
  2. Tie all found PCPs along the street.
    • Palm Bay The City of Palm Bay requires a tie to the nearest intersection.
  3. Tie all found property corners of adjacent lots.
  4. Reset property corners at coordinates provided by the office or if a plat is provided.

Set a nail at the center of the subject lot on the oppisite side of the road. This allows construction vehicles to enter and exit without disturbing the point.

  1. Locate all improvements on the subject lot starting at the centerline of the road (including but not limited to AP, fences, risers, power poles, overhead power, etc).
  2. Ground Shots on a 50 foot grid if possible.
    1. Palm Coast City of Palm Coast rquires a 25 foot grid across the entire lot.
    2. Palm Bay City of Palm Pay rquires a 25 foot grid along the property lines.
  3. Locate all drainage structures within 100 feet (including MES' and culverts).
  4. Locate finish floor elevations of adjacent houses if a house is built.
    1. Palm Bay | VLE | PSL Get the FFE of adjacent houses two lots away and three FFEs behind the subject property.
  5. Locate sanitary manholes or septic on adjacent lots. Locate water meters or wells on adjacent lots
  6. Deltona Locate all trees that are 6" and above

Find or set a nail in the nearest power pole. If a power pole does not exist within 100 feet, you may use the rim of a manhole, set an "X" cut in a drainage structure, or use a fire hydrant bolt.

Palm Coast The City of Palm Coast strongly recommends setting a nail in a power pole, even one across the road or a few lots away.

Palm Bay Locate the closest Palm Bay benchmark. If not provided, call the office or use this website to locate the closest benchmark.

Palm Bay Locate or set two nails in the road in front of the subject lot, one in front of each property corner.

If you have any questions, please call Steve or the office.

Office Note: If a lot is on a curve, it is generally harder to tie the boundary to the monuments. Please take extra considerations when working on curved lots.
