A. Modelspace Requirements

Import the field data in PNEZD (comma-delinted) point format. Verify that the points are in the correct location either by using the Geolocaton feature or verifying against known points.

Run the UPDATEALLPOINTGROUPS command to ensure that the Civil 3D point groups display points as required.

B. Boundary Survey Requirements

Determine the basis of bearing. Move the linework onto the points and rotate to your bearing basis. See this article for help with this process.

Do not move points under any circumstance. Move the linework onto the points.

Using the appropriate layers, breaklines, and crows feet, depict the location of the second point for the basis of bearing. Annotate the second point in accordance with section (B)(3) of this part.

Add measured bearings and distances to boundary segment labels (use plat bearings and rotation). Add appropriate blocks to corners and add descriptions.

C. Topographic Survey Requirements

Verify your ground shots, edge of pavement shots, and centerline shots. If required by the municipality, download, insert, and verify a lidar is within tolerance of our data. When in doubt, use our data.

Draft any linework that is required, including but not limited to the edge of pavement, sidewalks, and fence lines.

If there are any encroachments onto our property, draw it, annotate a distance from the point or furthest point on the polygon to the boundary. Inform the PSM in either the comment section or in-person about the encroachment.

EXCEPTION: Utility items such as meters, guy anchors, fences, and risers do not qualify as encroachments

If required by the municipality, show tree locations using the appropriate block and noting the size and type next to the block.

If the property lies within two (2) or more flood zones, depict the line(s) and annotate both sides of each line.

D. Paperspace Requirements

Unlock the viewport and change the view to be centered on the subject property and surrounding information. Choose a scale that best fits the lot and information, preferring being closer to a 1" = 1' scale. Ensure no text, symbols, or other relevatant data is cut or outside of the viewport. Change the scale in the top-right of the template to match your viewport scale by using the selection attribute (downwards triangle).

To the left of the company logo on the template, take the "BOUNDARY SURVEY" text object and place it under the logo and horizontally centered under the logo. If the topographic survey section was performed, change the text to read "BOUNDARY & TOPOGRAPHIC SURVEY".

Fill out Surveyor's Note 2 (orange section) with the date of the field data.

Perform a 30 second look over of all the information, including items you may not have changed.
