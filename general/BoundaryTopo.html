A. Boundary Survey Requirements

Determine the basis of bearing. Move the linework onto the points and rotate to your bearing basis. See this article for help with this process.

Never move cogo points. Always move linework onto the points.

Using the appropriate layers, breaklines, and crows feet, depict the location of the second point for the basis of bearing. Annotate the second point in accordance with section (B)(3) of this part.

Add measured bearings and distances to boundary segment labels (use plat bearings and rotation). Add appropriate blocks to corners and add descriptions.

B. Topographic Survey Requirements

Verify your ground shots, edge of pavement shots, and centerline shots. If required by the municipality, download, insert, and verify a lidar is within tolerance of our data. When in doubt, use our field data over lidar.

Draft any linework that is required, including but not limited to the edge of pavement, sidewalks, and fence lines.

If there are any encroachments onto our property, draw it, annotate a distance from the point or furthest point on the polygon to the boundary. Inform the PSM in either the comment section or in-person about the encroachment.

EXCEPTION: Utility items such as meters, guy anchors, fences, and risers do not qualify as encroachments

If the property lies within two (2) or more flood zones, depict the seperation line(s) and annotate both sides of each line.

C. Paperspace Requirements

Unlock the viewport and change the view to be centered on the subject property and surrounding information. Choose a scale that best fits the lot and information, preferring being closer to a 1" = 1' scale. Ensure no text, symbols, or other relevant data is cut or outside of the viewport. Change the scale in the top-right of the template to match your viewport scale by using the selection attribute (downwards triangle).

To the left of the company logo on the template, take the "BOUNDARY SURVEY" text object and place it under the logo and horizontally centered under the logo. If the topographic survey section was performed, change the text to read "BOUNDARY & TOPOGRAPHIC SURVEY".

Fill out the missing sections in the Surveyor's Notes.

Surveyor's Note 1
Fill out the missing information about your basis of bearing, including what you held, the bearing, and where the bearing came from (typically plat bearing).
Surveyor's Note 2
Add the date of the field survey to the the end of the note. If the field data has been revised, add a new date behind the existing date(s).
